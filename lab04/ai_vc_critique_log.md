### 1. THE 8-SECOND TEST

### 2. THE INSIGHT TEST
**The Insight:** Using a Chinese-native engine (Qwen-2) to solve for "business subtext" and "slang" that US-based models (like GPT-4) miss.
*   **Is it non-obvious?** It’s *borderline*. Most founders claim "my prompt is better." You are claiming "my engine is geographically/culturally superior."

### 3. THE OPENAI THREAT
**The Moat:** Currently, your moat is a "thin layer" of latency optimization and a specific LLM choice.
*   **The Reality:** If OpenAI releases a "Global Business Voice Mode" with localized low-latency servers in Singapore next week, you are dead.
*   **The Push:** "Choosing Qwen-2 is a configuration, not a moat." Your real moat must be the **Data Flywheel**—the proprietary dataset of *Vietnamese-accented* Chinese errors and specific trade-related roleplays that no US company cares enough to scrape. ### 4. THE NUMBERS TEST
